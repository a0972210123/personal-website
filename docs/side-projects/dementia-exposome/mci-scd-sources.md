# MCI / SCD prevalence sources (global deep-research)

*Cloud deep-research output for the dementia-exposome map. Machine-readable twin: `scripts/mci-scd-sources.json`. Method: 66-agent Tier A per-country + Tier B regional WebSearch fan-out (2026-07-16), per `cloud-research-brief-mci-scd.md`. Values extracted from abstracts / OA full text — **modelled, heterogeneous, indicative only; do not compare across countries.***

**Coverage:** 97 countries with data · **MCI 92** · **SCD 38**. Countries absent here fall back to the Bai 2022 WB-region MCI baseline already in the map; SCD has no map layer yet.

## Reading the table
- **Criteria/tool matters**: `MoCA` overestimates MCI (~20%+), `MMSE`/`CIND` differ, `Petersen`/`DSM-5 mild NCD` are the diagnostic standard. SCD `self-report-single-item` inflates (40-60%) → `low` confidence.
- **Access**: `OA` = open full text · `govt` = government page · `paywalled(pub)` = value read from abstract, verify at fetch.
- **Sub-nat ✓** = admin-1/province/state breakdown exists (see the JSON's `subnational` for the unit level).

## Regional MCI/SCD notes (Tier B long-tail baselines)

- **East Asia & Pacific** — Best regional MCI pooled anchor: Southeast Asia MCI ~17.08% in older adults, from "Emerging trends in cognitive impairment and dementia among older populations in Asia: a systematic review," Journal of Global Health 2024;14:04233 (https://jogh.org/2024/jogh-14-04233; PMC11544519) — but SE Asia is severely data-sparse (only 2 of 39 included studies were Southeast Asian). The SEACURE consensus (2025, Southeast Asia) explicitly states there is no official regional MCI estimate. No SCD-specific regional meta-analysis covers these countries. Pacific islands (Fiji, Papua New Guinea, Solomon Islands, Vanuatu, New Caledonia), Timor-Leste, Cambodia, and North Korea have NO country-specific MCI or SCD prevalence; Western Pacific dementia data is sparse and heterogeneous (Lancet Regional Health – Western Pacific 2024, "Risk factors for the neurodegenerative dementias in the Western Pacific region," PIIS2666-6065(24)00045-2; PMC11471060). SCD prevalence is absent across ALL listed countries. Country records below (Laos, Myanmar, Brunei, Mongolia) use screening-tool "cognitive impairment" figures (HDS-R / Mini-COG / local scales) as MCI proxies — none are DSM-5/Petersen MCI, so all are confidence=low and likely overestimate true MCI while conflating dementia-range cases.
- **Central Asia & Caucasus** — No dedicated MCI/SCD meta-analysis exists for Central Asia & the Caucasus; the region is largely uncovered by dementia epidemiology. The only genuinely multi-country regional data is the preliminary Kazakh–Kyrgyz comparison (Int Psychogeriatrics 2020, abstract 338, https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/international-psychogeriatrics/article/338-risk-factors-for-moderate-cognitive-impairment-and-dementia-in-the-kazakh-and-kyrgyz-populations-preliminary-results/FDA10846FCD955E4AD9F9029368E6A1F). For pooled fallback use the global community-dwelling estimate: Bai W, et al. Worldwide prevalence of MCI among community dwellers aged 50+: meta-analysis. Age Ageing 2022;51(8):afac173 — pooled MCI 15.56% (https://academic.oup.com/ageing/article/51/8/afac173/6670563). A nursing-home MCI meta-analysis grouped 'Europe & Central Asia' at ~19.7% (institutional) vs ~10.9% community (Transl Psychiatry 2023, https://www.nature.com/articles/s41398-023-02361-1). No SCD prevalence data was located for any country in this region. Azerbaijan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan have no usable country-specific MCI/SCD prevalence and fall back to the regional/global pooled estimate.
- **Europe (tail)** — Primary multi-country source: SHARE (Survey of Health, Ageing and Retirement in Europe), 2022 wave — harmonized MCI + dementia estimates for 27 European countries + Israel (n=47,773, age 65+), validated against HCAP. MCI averaged ~24% (range 17.2% Sweden to 31.1% Portugal); highest ~30% ("almost a third") in Bulgaria, Cyprus, Greece, Portugal; lowest ~17% in Austria/Germany/Sweden/Denmark/Switzerland. This is the fallback pooled source for the in-scope SHARE countries lacking a national study — Estonia (ee), Croatia (hr), Lithuania (lt), Latvia (lv), Slovenia (si), Slovakia (sk): their exact per-country MCI values are in the paper's supplement (not in the abstract — need full text) and sit near the 24% mean. TRAP: SHARE MCI is screening-derived (SHARE-Cog 3-item) and likely OVERESTIMATES true clinical (Petersen) MCI. Citation: Bacher/Börsch-Supan et al., "Harmonized prevalence estimates of dementia in Europe vary strongly with childhood education," Scientific Reports 2025, https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-97691-z (PMID 40269049; PMC12019132). Global MCI triangulation for fallback: pooled 19.7% (Frontiers Aging Neurosci 2023, PMC10641463) and 23.7% (BMC Geriatrics 2025, PMID 40329163). NO usable MCI/SCD prevalence found for Bosnia-Herzegovina (ba), Belarus (by), Moldova (md), Montenegro (me), North Macedonia (mk), Kosovo (xk), Serbia (rs), or Greenland (gl): Serbia has only self-reported memory/concentration difficulty (2019 national health survey, PMC11266187 — not diagnosed MCI); Greenland has a dementia register (254 cases, 2011) + RUDAS/Mini-Cog validation (PMC8245097) but no MCI/SCD rate; the others appear only in Alzheimer Europe / CEE dementia (not MCI) meta-analyses. No multi-country structured SCD-I estimate exists for the region — only Luxembourg MemoVie complaints (26%).
- **Latin America & Caribbean (tail)** — Regional pooled MCI estimate (use as fallback for countries with no usable specific value): "The prevalence of mild cognitive impairment in Latin America and the Caribbean: a systematic review and meta-analysis", Aging & Mental Health 2022;26(9), DOI 10.1080/13607863.2021.2003297 (OA, PMC9466284). 11 studies, n=20,220, 9 countries (Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Colombia, Peru, Cuba, Dominican Republic, Venezuela, Costa Rica). All-type MCI pooled range 6.8–25.5%; amnestic MCI 3.1–10.5%; prevalence rises with age and falls with education. Second useful multi-country source: 10/66 Dementia Research Group (Sosa 2012, PLOS Med, DOI 10.1371/journal.pmed.1001170) — strict Mayo amnestic MCI (aMCI) across Cuba/DR/Venezuela/Puerto Rico/Peru/Mexico: crude 0.8–4.3%, standardized 0.6–4.6% (underestimates all-type MCI). No comparable LAC-wide SCD meta-analysis exists; SCD figures are single-country and self-report-inflated (40–60% range), so treat with low confidence.
- **Middle East & North Africa (tail)** — MCI/SCD-specific regional pooled data for the MENA tail are sparse (GBD does NOT produce MCI/SCD). Closest MCI-specific regional meta-analysis: Asnakew et al., "Magnitude and predictors of mild cognitive impairment among older populations in Africa: a systematic review and meta-analysis," Translational Psychiatry 2025 (36 studies, 2005-2023) — pooled MCI prevalence ~26.78% for 2023 among older Africans; covers the North-African tail (Sudan, Algeria, Libya, Tunisia). https://www.nature.com/articles/s41398-025-03620-z (DOI 10.1038/s41398-025-03620-z). For dementia triangulation across the whole MENA set (dementia, NOT MCI): Sedighi et al., "Prevalence of dementia in selected Middle East and North Africa (MENA) countries: A systematic review and meta-analysis," Alzheimer's & Dementia Jan 2026 — pooled dementia 12.16% (95% CI 9.61-14.96), 52 studies / 1,045,908 people (Israel 17.0%, Iran 13.2%, Turkey 11.4%, Saudi 8.34%, Egypt 6.86%). https://alz-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/alz.71109 (DOI 10.1002/alz.71109). No MENA-wide SCD meta-analysis identified; single-item subjective-memory measures in the region (Lebanon LSAHA 56.3%; Türkiye 61%) inflate SCD and are low-confidence. Country records for ly=Libya, tn=Tunisia, om=Oman, qa=Qatar, sy=Syria are omitted — no usable country-specific community MCI/SCD prevalence found (only hospital dementia case series or MoCA-norm/validation samples); these fall back to the regional pooled estimate.
- **West & Central Africa** — Best regional pooled estimate for fallback: "Cognitive Ageing in Africa: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Pre-Dementia Prevalence and Associated Factors" (EA Health Research Journal, 2025) — 28 studies, 9 African countries, n=26,067; pooled MCI 20% (95% CI 14-28%, n=23,749) and SCD 50% (95% CI 40-59%, n=2,318). Extreme heterogeneity (I²≈99%): studies using neuropsychological cut-offs alone reported ~3x higher MCI than those applying clinical criteria — key methodological caveat for this region. URL: https://eahrj.eahealth.org/eah/article/view/840 . Corroborating: "Magnitude and predictors of mild cognitive impairment among older populations in Africa: a systematic review and meta-analysis" (Translational Psychiatry, Oct 2025) pooled Africa MCI ~26.4% (95% CI 15.4-41.6%), https://www.nature.com/articles/s41398-025-03620-z . Francophone context: "Dementia and cognitive impairment in French-speaking Sub-Saharan Africa: a comprehensive review" (2023) reports population cognitive-impairment prevalence ranging 10.4% (rural Benin) to 37.9% (CAR survey); DSM-IV dementia 6.4%, 10/66 dementia 21.6% — https://scholarworks.utrgv.edu/som_pub/1078/ . NOTE: No country-specific SCD prevalence exists for any country in this region — all SCD falls back to the regional pooled ~50%. GBD does not produce MCI/SCD.
- **East & Southern Africa** — Regional pooled fallback: East Africa scoping review of population-based studies (Yenesew MA et al., J Alzheimers Dis 2024;100(4)) reports cognitive impairment 7–44% and dementia 6–23% among adults aged ≥50–70 — but only 3 CI + 4 dementia population studies exist for all of East Africa, so data are extremely scarce. URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11380225/ (OA). Triangulate with: (a) Africa-wide MCI systematic review & meta-analysis (Translational Psychiatry 2025, doi s41398-025-03620-z, https://www.nature.com/articles/s41398-025-03620-z) — exact pooled % not captured in abstract, need full text; (b) LMIC MCI systematic review (McGrattan AM et al., J Alzheimers Dis 2021;79(2):743-762, https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33361599/) which incorporates the Tanzania Hai data. SCD: no structured SCD-I prevalence exists for ANY country in this region; the only self-report figures (Malawi 66%, Botswana census 22%) are single-item / disability-construct measures and are inflated/mismatched — low confidence. For the ~13 countries with nothing (Angola, Burundi, Djibouti, Eritrea, Madagascar, Mozambique, Namibia, Rwanda, Somalia, South Sudan, Eswatini, Zimbabwe, Lesotho) use the regional pooled estimate.
- **South Asia (tail)** — No country-specific MCI or SCD prevalence exists for Afghanistan or Bhutan; both should fall back to a South Asia regional pooled MCI estimate. Best regional coverage: Islam MR et al., "Emerging trends in cognitive impairment and dementia among older populations in Asia: A systematic review," J Glob Health 2024;14:04233 (https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC11544519/) — of 39 eligible 2019-23 studies, only 7 were from South Asia and 0 from Afghanistan/Bhutan, confirming a data void; it reports cognitive-impairment/dementia trends, not a South-Asia pooled MCI point estimate. Global pooled MCI ~19.7% for triangulation: Bai W et al., Front Aging Neurosci 2023;15:1227112 (https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10641463/). Nearest South-Asian MCI point estimate is a 2023 Delhi community study (~9.35%, HMSE+MoCA, India). AFGHANISTAN — the ONLY attributable cognitive data is a DEMENTIA study, not MCI/SCD: Trani JF et al., "Multidimensional poverty is associated with dementia among adults in Afghanistan," eClinicalMedicine 2023;58:101906, DOI 10.1016/j.eclinm.2023.101906 (https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36969341/); cross-sectional survey, n=478 adults aged 50+, Badakhshan & Ghazni provinces (Feb-Apr 2022), CIDAPSA project, using the Rowland Universal Dementia Assessment Scale (RUDAS). It stages dementia (mild vs moderate-to-severe) and does not report an MCI or SCD prevalence; RUDAS over-identifies impairment in low-literacy populations, so its figures are not usable as MCI. A separate mental-health survey cited ~5.8% dementia among Afghans 65+ (dementia, not MCI). BHUTAN — no MCI, SCD, or dementia prevalence study located (neighbouring Nepal ~8.8-10.7% dementia and Bangladesh data exist, but nothing for Bhutan); rely on regional pooled fallback.
